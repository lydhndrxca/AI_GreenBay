2350 E Mason St · Free entry (mall) · Green Bay’s only enclosed mall
27 stores and services. Inside: Castlecade family amusement center with classic video arcade games and pinball. Good rainy-day option, especially with kids.
East River Trail
Bellevue · Free · 2-mile multi-use trail
Two-mile paved trail along the East River in Bellevue. Parking off Westminster Drive. Walking, running, biking. A quieter alternative to the Fox River Trail.
Public Swimming
Green Bay Outdoor Pools (3 Locations)
Colburn Pool, Joannes Aquatic Center, Resch Aquatic Center · $5/person · Season pass $25
Three public outdoor pools. Colburn Pool: 50-meter Olympic-size, diving boards, heated 79–89°F. Joannes & Resch: zero-depth entry (great for kids). Season pass ($25) covers all three pools. Lap swim hours available. Open mid-June through Labor Day.
Josten Park
Bellevue · Free · Community park
Playgrounds, nature trails, sports fields. Hosts outdoor movies and concerts in summer. Nice local park away from the downtown crowds.
Lambeau & Ashwaubenon
Curly’s Pub
Lambeau Field Atrium · $$–$$$ · Year-round
Year-round restaurant and sports bar inside the Lambeau Field Atrium, named for Packers founder Curly Lambeau. Seats 530. “The Lambeau Heap” (1-lb BBQ bacon curd burger), hand-breaded cheese curds, steaks, seafood. Interactive football simulation games. Sun–Thu 11 AM–10 PM, Fri–Sat 11 AM–Midnight.
Packers Pro Shop
Lambeau Field Atrium · Free to browse · 21,500 sq ft
The largest in-store selection of Packers gear in the U.S. 6-ft replica helmet display, 10-ft football light fixture, refurbished 1957 Packers truck. Jerseys, hats, home goods, tailgating accessories, collectibles. Even non-fans will enjoy the spectacle.
The Bar — Holmgren Way
2001 Holmgren Way · $ · Sports bar
4.5-star sports bar near Lambeau. Bacon Jam Burger, Bam Bam Chicken Wrap, wings, pizza. Happy hour and daily drink specials. Great pre/post-game stop. Mon–Sat 11 AM–late.
Ashwaubenon Performing Arts Center
Ashwaubenon · Ticketed · Community PAC
Community performing arts center hosting concerts, magic shows, tribute acts, and school productions. Smaller and more intimate than the Resch. Check ashwaubenonpac.org for the current season.
Homefield Pub + Social / Topgolf Swing Suite
1025 Lombardi Ave, Suite 120 · $$ · Virtual golf & games
Restaurant and bar with Topgolf Swing Suites: virtual golf simulators plus Zombie Dodgeball, Hockey Shots, Quarterback Challenge, Skee Ball, darts, and more. Monday deal: 1 free hour with $25 food/bev purchase. Mon–Thu 11–9, Fri–Sat 11–10, Sun 11–8.
Recreation & Active Adventures
Odyssey Climbing + Fitness
686 Mike McCarthy Way (near Lambeau) · $25 day pass · 12,000 sq ft
10,000 sq ft of climbing space: 40+ roped belay bars, bouldering, plus a yoga studio and full fitness center. Guided gym visits and belay classes for beginners. Great rainy-day activity. Mon–Sat 9 AM–10 PM, Sun 9 AM–6 PM.
Fox River Kayaking Co. & Foxy Paddler
Fox River launches · $70/person guided · Seasonal
Guided kayak tours on the Fox River and into Green Bay. Stable sit-on-top kayaks, all levels welcome. Also: Foxy Paddler offers 2-hour paddle tours departing from Leicht Park. Sunset paddles available.
Hang Loose Rentals
Various launch points · $15/hr kayak, $360+ pontoon half-day · Seasonal
Kayak and paddleboard rentals ($15/hr, $50/day) with free delivery to boat launches in Green Bay, Howard, De Pere, and Suamico. Also rent pontoons ($360–$700). Explore the Fox River and bay at your own pace.
Duck Creek Golf Center
345 Village Ct · $ · Mini golf + driving range
18-hole mini golf course with waterfalls, spray fountains, and gardens. Also: 50-station driving range and practice putting green. Fun for families and casual golfers. (920) 498-2797.
The Wood Golf Course
530 Erie Rd · $ · Public course + driving range
Affordable public golf course with driving range (open 7 days, weather permitting). Buckets $6–$10. Two practice greens. Low-key 18 holes on Green Bay’s east side.
Kastle Karts
2301 N Irwin Ave (across from Bay Beach) · $7.99/race · Seasonal
Quarter-mile go-kart track with 30-degree banked turns and a drive-thru tunnel. Riders must be 56”+. Also: arcade room, concessions. 2-hour unlimited pass $59.99. Open daily 10 AM–9 PM in summer (Jun–early Sep). Birthday parties and group events. Great paired with Bay Beach next door.
Get Air Trampoline Park
Green Bay · $ · Indoor family fun
Wall-to-wall trampolines, dodgeball, foam pit, ninja obstacles. Kiddie Court for under 46”. Special events: Toddler Time (Tue/Thu 10 AM–Noon, Sun 8–10 AM), Club Air (Fri/Sat nights), special needs sessions (first Saturday monthly).
Packers Heritage Trail
Downtown GB & De Pere · Free · Walking/biking/driving
25 bronze commemorative plaques at historic Packers sites: birthplace of the Packers, Curly Lambeau’s office/birthplace, old City Stadium, Vince Lombardi’s home. Three sections: City Walk (16 sites), Packing Plant Spur (4), Lambeau-Lombardi Spur (4). Also available as a 90-min guided trolley tour on game weekends.
Secret Food Tours — Green Bay
Downtown · $$$ · Guided walking food tour
Guided walking food tour through Green Bay sampling local favorites. Group and private options. A great way to experience the city’s food scene with a knowledgeable local guide.
Hinterland Brewery Tour
1001 Lombardi Ave (Titletown) · By reservation · Includes souvenir glass + beer
Private brewery tours at the award-winning Hinterland Brewery across from Lambeau. See where craft beers are made, learn the process, and enjoy a tasting. Email info@hinterlandbeer.com to book.

Logistics Cheat Sheet

Downtown Parking
Multiple ramps and surface lots downtown. Street parking available with meters (free evenings & weekends). The Pine Street ramp is central.
Lambeau Field / Titletown Parking
Multiple lots around Lambeau. Lot 13 (off Mike McCarthy Way) for training camp. Private lots on Lombardi Ave for game days. Arrive early.
Resch Center Parking
Large lots adjacent to the Resch. Typically included with event ticket or $5–$10. Very accessible from Lombardi Ave.
Transit
Green Bay Metro bus system serves the city. Routes and schedules at greenbaywi.gov/metro. Uber/Lyft available.
Broadway District
Free surface parking along Broadway and side streets. The heart of the Farmers’ Market, restaurants, and shops. Very walkable.
Weather Notes
March–April: 30s–50s °F, layers recommended. May–June: 50s–70s. July–August: 70s–85s with humidity. Always have a rain layer for outdoor events.
